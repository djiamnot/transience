Rhythms

These cards offer all rhythmical possibilities. The brackets around the line
cut-out the rhythmical phrases you can use. Each rhythmical phrase can be
repeated maximum 9 times. But you can also play the entire line, up to 9
times. Rhythm lines come in two varieties: with note-stems and without.

RHYTHMS with STEMS

describe rhythms to a clear pulsation, which should also be audible as
organized rhythms. Of course, there is no overall tempo, nor the two of you
have an common tempo. The pulsation, therefore, is one you feel inside,
responding to the music you have played before and the Movement Mode that
is currently in force. You take this pulsation as q and play the rhythm
strictly to this pulse.

and less accented, big notes are longer and more accented â€“ but precisely

perceivable.

Thus the difference between the two rhythmical modes (pulsation and
free-floating) should be very clear to the listener.
