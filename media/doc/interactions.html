Recitation / Playing technique

These cards describe the way you as a duo interact with eachother. One of you is
the dotted line, the other the straight line (depending on who starts the interaction).
The lines do NOT describe melodic movements, just types of gesture (a jagged line is
more aggressive, a flowing line more organic, a straight line represents not a bordun
or no events, but just ANY kind of music that fits etc.).

Accompany: One plays in the background the other more in the
foreground. (Difference to trope: accompaniment can be more
flexible and include many different ways of playing)
Conversation: Both exchange that build upon each other each
keeping to their own mood, as in a real conversation
(Difference to trade: the fragments are longer, difference to quarrel:
the melodies fit to each other)
Destroy: One creates a mood which the other radically destroys.
Quietness destroys Joy, aggressiveness destroys harmony etc.
Then the destroyer creates a mood that the other destroys and so on.
Echoes: One tries to echo the other perfectly (pitches, rhythms
etc.) This is not reall possible, because transients and sound
qualities are different, but one can try. For the duration of
this mode the follower does not play her/his Rhythms and Melos models.
Embellish: One plays slowly and the other uses his pitches to
ornament the other‚Äùs melody in much faster notes.
Parallel: like echoes, only that you try to really do the same
thing at the same time. An exercise in absolute attention and little
signs. One of you again forgets about their own R and M models.
Quarrel: create a musical chaos, deliberately avoiding any
harmony or rhythmical coherence.
Trade (or b>Hoquetus): Quick alternations of mini-phrases
(1-3 notes).
Trope: One player creates a bordun or a repetitive pattern on
which the other can improvise. Exchange roles.
