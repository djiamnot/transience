Glissandi / Pitch Bend

These cards describe how you should pitch bend all important notes and most transient notes, too. These pitch bends can be fast or slow, depending on the length of the note. They need not go anywhere, but of course it would be optimal if they ended on one of the transient or stable pitches.


Stright gliss down
brief up-down or down-up gliss (evade-gliss)
hectic skipping on a note (as a stone on water) before down (or up) gliss
smooth swooping down gliss
smooth swooping up gliss
stright up gliss
irregular and unstable wobble down and back up
irregular and unstable wobble up and back down
No pitch bending
