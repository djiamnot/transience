Melos

These cards offer all the melos phrases you can use – either the whole line
or the shorter phrases cut-out by the brackets around the stave.

Each phrase consists of a minimum of three notes. The first, the last and one
or more of the middle notes can be considered “important”notes. (these notes
have fixed transients as explained on the appendix page) You can read each
phrase as often as you want, you can linger on each note or sequence as long
as it makes musical sense.

If your instructions in B are OFFPITCH the individual notes may be played
with microtonal variants, while keeping the general shape of the melos
phrase. Of course, each note can and often must be pitch-bent (according to D)
– but it should always be clear that you either start or end on one of the
pitches in your melos phrase. The koto can also play chords. In this case,
only one note of the chord will be pitch-bent.
